It is the intention that the IMRS is developed as a true international collaboration, for the following reasons:

  • To reduce the financial burden on any one nation.

  • To open up Mars for all humanity, rather than only one nation or ideology.

  • To improve international relations by further developing trust, communication and cooperation between the world’s major powers, thereby bringing about world peace.

  • To gain greater leverage from the research, expertise, designs and philosophies of the most talented scientists, engineers and managers from all over Earth, thus advancing space technology further and more quickly, reducing costs, benefiting Earth and all humanity, and increasing the overall value of the project’s outcomes.

 

Expanding the international partnership of the ISS

The ISS has been a huge success in terms of scientific research as well as international collaboration. The ISS partnership of 5 leading space agencies provides an excellent basis for expansion into a larger partnership for development of the IMRS.

The proposed new partners include:

  1. Brazil
  2. China
  3. India
  4. South Korea
  5. Iran

All of these either:

  • already have an association with the ISS; or
  • discussions have already begun about including them in the ISS partnership; or
  • they will have human spaceflight capability by about 2020

 

Ten partners

If this ambitious collaboration can be achieved, we will have ten international partners. The level of financial participation is roughly organised into three tiers, which determines the number of astronauts each nation can send within the first three missions. It is hoped and expected that future missions will be progressively cheaper and many other nations will also be able to send astronauts to Mars.

Crew slots

All international partners will be represented by at least one astronaut. This will give each partner a greater stake in the program, a greater return on investment in terms of national pride and recognition, and first-hand experience of a deep space, interplanetary mission.
